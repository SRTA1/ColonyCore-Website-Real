The Problem: Manual Flight Logging is Painful

Part 107 requires detailed flight records, but most operators:

  • Fill out paper logbooks after the fact (and forget details)
  • Maintain separate spreadsheets for each aircraft
  • Can't easily search historical flights
  • Scramble during insurance audits or FAA inspections
  • Don't track equipment hours for maintenance schedules

Manual logging takes 5-10 minutes per flight and is prone to errors. For operations running 20+ flights per week, that's 2+ hours of pure overhead.

How ColonyCore Flight Logs Work

1. Automatic Capture

Pilots log flights from mobile or desktop with pre-filled job and equipment data.

  • Job automatically selected from schedule
  • Aircraft and pilot pre-populated
  • Start/end time tracked with one tap
  • GPS location captured automatically
  • Weather conditions pulled from API

2. Compliance-Ready Records

Every log includes FAA-required information plus operational details.

  • Date and time of flight
  • Takeoff and landing location
  • Remote pilot name and certificate number
  • Aircraft registration and serial number
  • Purpose of flight (tied to job)

  • Any incidents or anomalies
